Glossary

Key terms and acronyms used throughout DOCSIS standards and this tool — hover underlined terms anywhere on the page for quick definitions

FDX
Full Duplex DOCSIS

Allows simultaneous upstream & downstream transmission on the same frequencies using a Network Echo Canceller (NEC) at the node. Eliminates the need for a physical split upgrade — the upstream can reuse the 108–684 MHz band that FDD plants dedicate entirely to downstream.

FDD
Frequency Division Duplex

Traditional cable broadband approach where upstream and downstream use separate, non-overlapping frequency bands. The split point (e.g. 85 MHz) is the hard boundary. Simple and robust — no echo cancellation required.

OFDM
Orthogonal Frequency Division Multiplexing

Downstream modulation method introduced in DOCSIS 3.1. Splits a wide channel (24, 96, or 192 MHz) into thousands of closely-spaced subcarriers (25 or 50 kHz each). Supports up to 4096-QAM and LDPC FEC, far exceeding legacy SC-QAM efficiency.

OFDMA
Orthogonal Frequency Division Multiple Access

Upstream counterpart to OFDM. Multiple cable modems share subcarriers in both time and frequency, enabling flexible per-modem bandwidth grants. The CMTS schedules which modems transmit on which subcarriers each interval.

A-TDMA
Advanced Time Division Multiple Access

Legacy upstream access method since DOCSIS 2.0. Modems share 6.4 MHz SC-QAM channels by transmitting in assigned time slots at 5.12 Msps. Still widely deployed alongside OFDMA for backward compatibility with older CPE.

SC-QAM
Single Carrier QAM

Traditional DOCSIS modulation using a single 6 MHz carrier. Downstream supports up to 256-QAM at 5.057 Msps. Used since DOCSIS 1.0 and still bonded alongside OFDM channels. Cannot use LDPC or high QAM orders — those require OFDM.

QAM
Quadrature Amplitude Modulation

Encodes data by simultaneously varying both amplitude and phase of a carrier. Higher orders pack more bits per symbol (4096-QAM = 12 bits/symbol) but require better signal quality (higher SNR). The order is always a power of 2.

LDPC
Low-Density Parity-Check

Forward error correction (FEC) code used in DOCSIS 3.1+. Provides near-Shannon-limit performance. The max coding rate is 0.94, meaning 94% of transmitted bits carry data and 6% are FEC overhead. SC-QAM uses simpler Reed-Solomon FEC.

HFC
Hybrid Fiber-Coaxial

The cable network infrastructure: optical fiber from headend to neighborhood nodes, then coaxial cable for the last mile to subscribers. The node is where the optical signal converts to RF on coax — and where FDX echo cancellers are installed.

ESD
Extended Spectrum DOCSIS

DOCSIS 4.0 feature extending upstream to 684 MHz via a high split, and downstream to 1794 MHz. Achieves 6 Gbps upstream through additional spectrum rather than echo cancellation. Requires amplifier and tap upgrades but not full fiber deep.

CMTS
Cable Modem Termination System

The headend equipment that manages all cable modems on the plant. Schedules upstream time slots and subcarrier grants, bridges between the HFC plant and IP backbone, and enforces QoS policies. Modern CMTS units handle OFDM/OFDMA alongside SC-QAM.

Split
Return Path Split Point

The frequency boundary dividing upstream from downstream spectrum. Common splits: Low (85 MHz) · Mid (204 MHz) · High (300 MHz) · ESD (684 MHz). Higher splits allocate more spectrum to upstream but require replacing diplex filters in every amplifier and tap on the coax plant.

Bonding
Channel Bonding

Combining multiple independent channels into one logical pipe to increase aggregate throughput. DOCSIS 3.0 introduced bonding — up to 32 DS / 8 US SC-QAM channels. DOCSIS 3.1 replaces many bonded SC-QAM channels with a single wide OFDM channel.

Msps
Symbol Rate (Mega-symbols/sec)

How fast the modulator changes state. Capacity = Symbol Rate × bits/symbol × coding rate. For OFDM, the symbol rate equals the subcarrier spacing (e.g. 25 kHz × 7,040 active subcarriers). For SC-QAM DS: 5.057 Msps; US A-TDMA: 5.12 Msps.

PNM
Proactive Network Management

DOCSIS 3.1/4.0 suite of tools for real-time plant monitoring: spectrum analysis, impulse noise detection, pre-equalization data, and topology mapping. Allows operators to identify and fix plant impairments before customers notice degraded service.

MULPI
MAC and Upper Layer Protocols Interface

The CableLabs specification document that defines the DOCSIS MAC layer. The D3.1 MULPI spec is the authoritative source for rules like "max 2 downstream OFDM channels per cable modem" and upstream OFDMA grant scheduling.

CableLabs
Cable Television Laboratories

Non-profit R&D consortium that develops and maintains DOCSIS, PacketCable, and other cable broadband specifications. Member operators (Comcast, Charter, Cox, etc.) fund the research. All DOCSIS specs are freely downloadable from cablelabs.com.
